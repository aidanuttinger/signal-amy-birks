Work through each dimension below. For each one, answer the questions in your own words. At the end, you'll have a first draft of your proprietary Integration Intelligence Diagnostic Framework — the thing that makes every conversation with a prospect feel like you already know their organization.

The 5 Dimensions
1
Leadership Alignment Reality
Are the senior teams genuinely aligned — or performing alignment?
The signals you're already reading
What leaders say in the room vs. what they say in the hallway
How quickly decisions get made — or re-opened
Whether both sides of the deal are actually in the room, or one side is performing for the other
The degree to which leaders can finish each other's sentences — or avoid completing each other's
Questions to unlock your version of this dimension
When you walk into a leadership session, what's the first thing you notice that tells you alignment is real vs. performed?
What does misalignment sound like at the leadership level — not the words, but the pattern?
What's the most dangerous form of false alignment you've encountered — and how did you spot it?
Your answer — in your own words This becomes your proprietary definition
2
Fear Index
What is the threat level in the room — and where is it distorting the plan?
The signals you're already reading
Who speaks and who goes quiet when leadership enters
The quality of questions being asked — or not asked
Whether people are protecting information or sharing it
How people talk about "the other side" of the deal when they think leadership isn't listening
Questions to unlock your version of this dimension
How do you distinguish productive tension from threat response in a room full of senior people?
What does fear look like in a CFO or Corporate Dev Director — it's not always obvious. What are your tells?
Where does fear show up FIRST in an integration — what's the leading indicator before it becomes visible?
Your answer — in your own words
3
Communication Health
Is information flowing cleanly — or being distorted, filtered, or weaponized?
The signals you're already reading
The gap between what leadership intends to communicate and what people actually receive
Whether the tone of communications activates safety or threat in the reader
How many layers a message passes through before it reaches front-line employees
The ratio of "here's what we know" to "here's what we don't know yet" in announcements
Questions to unlock your version of this dimension
When you read a client's integration communications before you've met anyone — what are the two or three things that immediately tell you something's wrong?
What does "communications that activate safety" actually look like vs. "communications that activate threat"? Can you give a specific example of each?
Your answer — in your own words
4
Cultural Compatibility & Distance
How different are the two cultures — and does anyone actually know?
The signals you're already reading
How each side talks about the other when the other isn't in the room
The degree to which each organization has explicit vs. unspoken norms
How decisions get made in each culture — consensus vs. hierarchy vs. speed
Whether leadership has done any deliberate cultural assessment or is "assuming fit"
Questions to unlock your version of this dimension
In your experience, what's the most common cultural mismatch that deal teams underestimate — and why?
What does "cultural distance" actually feel like in a room? How do you sense it before anyone names it?
Your answer — in your own words
5
Execution Ownership
Who actually owns this integration — and do they believe it will work?
The signals you're already reading
Whether the people responsible for execution were involved in designing the plan
The degree to which ownership is distributed vs. concentrated at the top
Whether frontline managers believe the strategy will actually work in practice
The gap between official accountability and real influence
Questions to unlock your version of this dimension
How do you know within the first session whether an integration team has real ownership vs. performative ownership?
What does it look like when the people executing the plan secretly don't believe it will work — and how do you surface that?
Your answer — in your own words
