Phase 1 · Opening
Set the Stage ~10 minutes
Goal: Build authority, create intrigue, set the frame for what's coming
Opening — Your Introduction
"Amy — before we get into your work, I want to give you a little context on who I am and how I see the world, because it'll make what we're about to do together make more sense.

I've spent most of my career doing one thing: finding the gap between where someone is positioned and where they could be. The gap between what makes them extraordinary and what they're actually saying to the world. Most people — even brilliant ones — are invisible in their own category because they're speaking the language of the category rather than the language of the people they're trying to reach.

I've done this across industries — for companies, consultants, nonprofits. I have a nose for it. And when I read your briefing, something jumped out at me immediately. You're not what you're calling yourself. And that gap — between what you actually do and how you're currently positioned — is the thing we're going to close today."
🎯 Your goal here: Create genuine curiosity. She should be leaning in before you've told her anything. The phrase "you're not what you're calling yourself" is designed to do that. Pause after it. Let it land.
Framing the Session
"Here's how this is going to go. I'm going to ask you some questions — some of them might feel unexpected. I'm not doing discovery for its own sake. I'm looking for something specific: the thing that makes you genuinely irreplaceable to the people who need you most. When I find it, you'll know.

And at the end of today's conversation, I'm going to show you something. A new version of your positioning — the website, the strategy, the tools that prove it — built specifically around what I've learned about you. So this isn't just a conversation. It's a working session. Let's go."
Introduced yourself and your positioning lens
Created intrigue around the gap between her positioning and her actual value
Set expectation that something will be revealed at the end

Phase 2 · Surface Discovery
Find the Gold ~25 minutes
Goal: Uncover the stories, specifics, and instincts that make Amy's methodology genuinely unique
Facilitation principle: Don't talk much here. Ask the question. Listen. Reflect back. Then go deeper. The gold is always one layer below the first answer. Every time she says something interesting, ask: "Say more about that." or "What does that actually look like in practice?"
Q1
Take me back to the moment — the earliest one you can remember — when you realized that you were seeing something in an organization that everyone else was missing. What was happening? What did you see?
Why this question: Gets her into a specific memory rather than a generalization. The story she tells here is likely her best origin story — and probably the most powerful version of what makes her different. Listen for the specific language she uses to describe what she saw.
If she goes abstract, push her into the specific:
  • "Give me the actual scene — where were you, who was in the room?"
  • "What was everyone else focused on while you were seeing this?"
  • "What happened next — when you named what you saw?"
Q2
You've described your "GPS-Brain" — the ability to walk into a room and feel where the friction is within 20 minutes. Walk me through that. What are you actually picking up on? What are the signals?
Why this question: This is the diagnostic framework in embryonic form. Every signal she names is a potential dimension of her proprietary assessment. Listen closely — this is the intellectual property she hasn't articulated yet.
Drilling deeper:
  • "Is it what people say, or is it what they don't say?"
  • "Can you name the thing you're looking for — even if it's hard to put into words?"
  • "If you had to build a checklist of what to look for in a room — what's on it?"
Q3
Tell me about a time your work made the biggest difference — the engagement where you can point to before and after and say: "that happened because I was there." What changed? What would have happened if you hadn't been there?
Why this question: This is the case study she needs. Also — the language she uses to describe the outcome is the language her prospects need to hear. Write down exact phrases.
Follow-up probes:
  • "How much do you think that outcome was worth in dollar terms — rough guess?"
  • "Does that client know what you actually did for them?"
  • "Would they go on record saying that? Could that become a case study?"
Q4
What's the most common thing you walk into that the organization doesn't even realize is a problem yet?
Why this question: This surfaces her proprietary insight — the pattern she recognizes that others don't. Whatever she says here is the heart of her diagnostic framework and probably the strongest message for her website.
Then ask:
  • "And when they realize it's a problem — how far along are they usually by then?"
  • "What does it cost them between the moment it starts and the moment they finally see it?"
Q5
Who is your absolute dream client? Not a category — a person. The specific type of human being who gets maximum value from working with you, where the work flows, where you feel most alive in the engagement.
Why this question: The texture of her answer will sharpen the target buyer section of the strategy. Also — her excitement level here tells you whether she's chasing the right buyer or the safe one.
Push for specificity:
  • "What's their title? What's keeping them up at 2am?"
  • "Have you had this client? Who was closest to this ideal?"
Q6
What do you never want to do again? The type of work, or the type of client, that leaves you feeling like a vendor rather than an advisor?
Why this question: Boundaries define positioning. What she wants to exclude is as important as what she wants to attract. This also reveals any mismatch between how she's currently positioned and where she wants to go.
Origin story captured — the moment she first saw what others missed

Phase 3 · Strategic Deep Dive
Challenge the Assumptions ~15 minutes
Goal: Surface what she hasn't said out loud — the hidden positioning and unspoken constraints
Shift in energy here: Move from warm discovery to sharper, more provocative questions. You're not being harsh — you're treating her like a strategic peer. This is where breakthroughs happen. The frame: "I'm going to push you a bit here."
Q7
What are you undercharging for? Where do you know — intuitively — that the value you deliver is an order of magnitude larger than the fee you're comfortable asking for?
Why this question: Almost every high-performing independent advisor undercharges. This surfaces pricing confidence gaps — and the answer usually reveals where she does her best work. Also opens the conversation about fee structures.
Follow up with:
  • "What stops you from charging what you know it's worth?"
  • "If you had the evidence — the ROI numbers — what would you charge?"
Q8
What's the thing you know about M&A integration that nobody else in this space is saying out loud? The inconvenient truth that most consultants won't touch?
Why this question: This is her thought leadership thesis. Whatever she says here becomes a LinkedIn post, a keynote, or a piece of content that establishes her as the category definer — not just a practitioner.
If she hesitates:
  • "You don't have to be diplomatic here. What do you actually believe that the industry pretends isn't true?"
  • "What would you say to a room of corporate development directors if you knew you'd never work with them again?"
Q9
What's the thing you're most afraid of getting wrong as you build this practice? The risk you're quietly managing?
Why this question: Surfaces her actual constraints and fears — which we need to address in the strategy. Also builds trust: you're not just here to pump her up, you're interested in the real picture.
Q10
If you had three clients like IQVIA running concurrently right now — full pipeline — what would you need to have in place that you don't have yet?
Why this question: This is the setup for the Snyper Agency offer. Her answer tells you exactly where the infrastructure gap is — and frames what you're about to offer as the specific solution to what she just named.
Listen for these gaps:
  • Lead generation / consistent pipeline
  • Marketing / positioning that reaches the right buyers
  • Back-end systems / automations / CRM
  • Credibility / case studies / social proof
  • Time — she can't do delivery AND business development
Q11
When I say "Integration Risk Intelligence" — the idea that your work sits upstream of change management as a financial risk mitigation play, not a culture program — how does that land for you?
Why this question: This is the positioning test. You're introducing the new frame and watching her reaction. If her eyes light up, you've found it. If she hesitates, you'll learn why — and you can adjust before the full reveal.
Listen for the energy in her response:
  • 🔥 Immediate "yes" energy → amplify it, go straight to the reveal
  • 🤔 Thoughtful hesitation → probe what's missing or what feels off
  • 😬 Resistance → ask what language SHE would use — then incorporate it
Pricing gap surfaced — where she knows she undercharges
Her provocative industry truth named — future thought leadership
Real constraints and fears on the table
Infrastructure gaps identified — sets up the Snyper offer
New positioning tested — her reaction noted
